<h3 align="center">Project Proposal: Predicting Hotel Occupancy Rate and Average Daily Rate in Antalya/Alanya Resort Hotels Using Machine Learning</h3>

**1. Motivation & Background**
 Having grown up around resort hotels in the Antalya/Alanya region of Turkey, I developed a strong interest in how hotel performance is shaped by both operational decisions and external market conditions. Since my father works as a General Manager in this sector, I have direct exposure to the dynamics of resort hotel management, particularly the balance between B2B agency sales and B2C direct demand. In this project, I aim to build a machine learning model that predicts two core business indicators: Occupancy Rate and Average Daily Rate in EUR (ADR). The broader goal is to understand how proprietary hotel performance data can be combined with external indicators to improve forecasting accuracy.
 
**2. Primary Data: Where Will I Get the Data and How Will I Collect It?**
 The main dataset will be obtained directly from the Property Management Systems (PMS) of three regional hotels: Side Mare, Azura, and Lago. These data will be collected through authorized exports from the hotels’ internal systems in CSV or Excel format. In addition, I will obtain a Nationality Breakdown Report to identify the dominant foreign markets, such as Germany, Russia, and the United Kingdom. This will provide useful contextual information for interpreting demand patterns across time.
 
**3. Data Characteristics**
 The primary dataset will consist of a daily or weekly time-series covering the last two to three years, with an estimated size of 1,000 to 2,500 observations. Key variables will include Net % Occupancy, and Sold Rooms. To enrich this dataset, I will merge three external sources: weekly Google Trends search data for tourism-related keywords such as “Antalya holiday,” historical EUR/TRY exchange rates downloaded from the TCMB EVDS portal, and possibly German/UK school holiday schedules converted into binary holiday indicators.
 
**4. Objective & Hypotheses**
 The objective of this project is to evaluate how digital demand, macroeconomic conditions, and calendar effects interact to influence hotel performance. H0: There is no significant correlation between Google Trends search volume, the EUR/TRY exchange rate, and the hotels’ daily occupancy and Euro-based revenue. H1: Variations in Google Trends search volume and the EUR/TRY exchange rate significantly affect the hotels’ daily occupancy rates and Euro-based ADR.
